Stephanie is a computer scientist in the Center for Applied Scientific Computing at Lawrence Livermore National Laboratory.

She received her Ph.D. and Masters in Computer Science from the University of Oregon in March 2019 and December 2016, respectively. She received her B.S. in Computer Engineering from the University of the Pacific in May 2013.

Her research interest focuses on power-constrained high performance computing and performance analytics. Stephanie's dissertation work focused on power/performance tradeoffs for scientific visualization workloads on supercomputers.

Software Projects

  • hatchet: Python-based tool for analyzing hierarchical performance data with pandas dataframes.
  • variorum: Hardware-agnostic library enabling control of underlying hardware features.
  • msr-safe: Kernel module providing whitelisted access to model-specific registers.
  • libmsr: C-based library providing control of Intel-specific hardware features encoded in model-specific registers, such as power capping.
